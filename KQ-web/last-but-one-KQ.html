Here is the last-but-one message from JB: [home]
Friday, July 26, 2002

Well, I guess that's it. I won't be finishing the game. I've been see-sawing between trying to complete it and just admitting that I have no interest in the game anymore. Well, I'm done trying to convince myself to finish it. I simply have no desire to work on this, or any other game, anymore. I no longer have the need to immerse myself in programming as a distraction from real life. Life is good again and I don't want to waste my time with stuff I am not interested in anymore.

I got into programming when I didn't have anyone to play AD&D with anymore. I wanted to take campaign ideas and characters and stuff and make a game. Well, I started doing that with Verge and The Silver Circle and then I made KQ to develop an engine for The Silver Circle and then KQ turned into a whole game on it's own. Well, now I'm going back to pen and paper. I've tried all different kinds of console and computer RPGs and although Morrowind comes close, no RPG has ever fully satisfied me like pen and paper RPGs do. No computer/console will ever duplicate the social element that real people add to an RPG... online RPGs can be pretty good, but it's still not good enough.

Whatever, I don't need to get too long-winded about all of this. I am packing up everything I have and putting in a big zip with some notes on how I had everything structured for development... it's pretty bad actually. All of the current source and stuff will be there, but the PCXs and WAVs and stuff will only be in the data files. You can extract them if you need to edit them. The only problem you may encounter is that I use perl for some parsing and miscellaneous crap. I taught myself perl for the purpose of helping with coding so it's pretty rough stuff.

Anyways, check the readme in the zip... enjoy... and sorry to all those people I told I would finish this. I guess, in hindsight, releasing this game to the public before it was done may not have been such a great idea. If I hadn't released it before it was done, at least I wouldn't feel bad about abandoning it.
